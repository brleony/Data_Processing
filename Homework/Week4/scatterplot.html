Tourism, Income Equality and GDP per capita in Europe in 2015

Data Processing Week 4 Assignment

Source: Eurostat

On the data:
Income equality is displayed on the y axis. It is measured by the gini coefficient, a measure that can range from 0 to 100. A value above 50 is considered high, while 30 or above is considered medium and lower than 30 is considered low.
The value on the x axis, tourism, represents the percentage of the total population of the country that went on a trip abroad of 4 days or longer.
The size of the dots is determined by the GDP (Gross Domestic Product) per capita, corrected for PPS (Purchasing Power Standards). PPS is fictive 'currency' units that remove differences in purchasing power, i.e. different price levels between countries.
The region classification is according to the United Nations.
